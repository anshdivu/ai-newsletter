Tips & Tricks
Build the context system before the prompt · The AI Corner
Context engineering replaced prompt engineering as the leverage point. What Claude receives, and in what order, matters more than how you phrase the ask. Start with three files: who you are, how you write, and a list of AI-isms Claude must never use in your voice. Power-user guide →
Give CLAUDE.md an instruction budget · TECHSY
Keep it under 200 lines. Shorter is better. Past roughly 80 lines, rules start dropping. Past 200, whole blocks get ignored. Treat every line as a cost on per-rule adherence and cut anything Claude already does by default. 9 rules for 2026 →
Hand Claude a feedback loop · Builder.io
Include the test command, the linter, or the expected output in your prompt. Claude runs the check, sees the failure, and fixes it without you stepping in. Practitioners call this the single biggest quality lever, worth a 2-3x improvement on its own. 50 field-tested tips →
Clear and restart after two wrong turns · Iwo Szapar
When Claude goes down the wrong path twice, stop correcting. Run /clear and restart with a sharper prompt that folds in what you learned. A fresh context with better instructions beats a long thread of patches. 8 rules learned the hard way →

Feature Spotlight
This week
Cowork, the Claude agent for everything that is not code
Cowork is Claude working across your files and apps instead of a repo. Point it at a folder, connect your tools, and hand it multi-step office work: reports, spreadsheets, decks, file cleanup. Anthropic's own data says over 90 percent of Cowork sessions are not software development. The biggest slice, at 33 percent, is business operations. As of this week it runs in the cloud too, so a task started at your desk keeps going after the laptop closes. Reach for it when the work spans files and apps, and for anything a chat window cannot finish alone.
